Reducing Water Waste in Daily Life

Every drop counts. Start saving water today.

The Problem

We often take water for granted. Letting the tap run while brushing our teeth, overwatering lawns, or ignoring leaks may seem minor, but these habits waste thousands of gallons each year. Together, they increase water bills and environmental harm.

Global Impact

Over two billion people live with high water stress. Scarce water affects health, food production, and daily life. By saving water at home, we help build a more sustainable future for everyone.

Did You Know?

  • Only 1% of the earth’s water is drinkable. The rest is salt water or frozen.
  • The human body is about 75% water, and we can only survive a few days without it.
  • Landscaping uses half of home water in California. Showers and toilets take most of the rest.
  • If every U.S. home had a faucet dripping once per second, we'd waste 928 million gallons daily.

How You Can Save Water

  • Fix leaks: Check under sinks or around pipes. Even small drips add up fast.
  • Turn off the tap: When brushing teeth or shaving, only use water when needed.
  • Use a bucket: Catch water while your shower warms up. Use it on plants.
  • Water wisely: Early morning or evening reduces loss from evaporation.
  • Install low-flow devices: These reduce usage without lowering performance.
  • Wait for full loads: Dishwashers and laundry machines waste less when full.
